Guidance: Doormat navigation

What are doormats?

Each link with its description provides an accurate and concise description of what a person will find on the destination page. Together, these sets of links and descriptions provide clear and distinct choices for people trying to complete their task.

Doormats replace the left-navigation menu.

Parks Canada guidance supersedes the Canada.ca guidance on: Services and information design pattern

Use:

  • Doormat text link labels must be consistent across all locations.
  • It is not mandatory to include all doormats; only include them when relevant. For the ones you do include, follow the associated guidance.
  • Before adding doormats other than the ones listed, contact the web team at contactweb@pc.gc.ca
  • Maximum number of doormats per heading: 9
Link text
  • These links may be featured elsewhere on the same page
  • Maximum characters:
    • 45 characters (including spaces) for both official languages (optimal)
    • Can extend to 75 characters (including spaces)
    • Description text
  • Recommended character limit: 120 characters (including spaces) in both official languages
  • Exception:
    • If description text is longer than 120 characters, this is acceptable as a placeholder for launch. Edit these descriptions to 120 characters as soon as possible.
    • Your main site navigation and top 10 pages should be updated for launch
  • Punctuation:
    • Use oxford commas for lists (in English only)
    • Finish with a period or other punctuation
  • Use action verbs, or list keywords to summarize the information or tasks that can be accomplished on the page
  • Consult the Content collection templates
  • Descriptive text is standard, with some optional items, and can be adapted to the location as needed

    Lake Superior National Marine Conservation Area

    Among the largest protected fresh-water areas, home to more than 70 fish species and 50 shipwrecks.

    Fort St. Joseph National Historic Site

    A vital British stronghold on Upper Canada's western front, on the southern tip of St. Joseph Island.

    Sault Ste. Marie Canal National Historic Site

    Learn about this innovative piece of engineering and its impact on water transportation in Canada.

    Fort Battleford National Historic Site

    Uncover the central but little-known role of Fort Battleford in the Conflict of 1885. Discover the stories of the First Nations, Métis, North West Mounted Police, and settlers in these difficult times.

    Prince Albert National Park

    Prince Albert National Park offers accessible wilderness and extensive outdoor recreation in central Saskatchewan. Hike boreal forests, canoe pristine lakes and see free-range bison, with the town of Waskesiu as a convenient base.

    Battle of Tourond's Coulee / Fish Creek National Historic Site

    This is the scene of an important victory for the Métis resistance in their fight against the Northwest Field Force of the Dominion of Canada. Discover the story of Fish Creek and the Battle of Tourond's Coulee.

    Grasslands National Park

    At Grasslands National Park, expanses of dinosaur fossils harken back to a time before history. Tipi rings are testament to First Nations communities, and ruins of prairie homesteads tell of settlers intent on taming the prairie.

    Fort Walsh National Historic Site

    Experience life with James Morrow Walsh and his NWMP troops in the 1870s. Barter for buffalo robes at the trading post, smell sweet lodge pole pines on a hike through the Cypress Hills.

    Motherwell Homestead National Historic Site

    Hear prairie songbirds sing and smell the sweet hay as you help feed the horses. Re-connect with Canada's homestead life and bake bread with flour ground from grain harvested from the golden fields outside.
