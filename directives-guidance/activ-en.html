Guidance: Doormat navigation - Non-symbol activities or experiencesOptional

What are doormats?

Each link with its description provides an accurate and concise description of what a person will find on the destination page. Together, these sets of links and descriptions provide clear and distinct choices for people trying to complete their task.

Doormats replace the left-navigation menu.

Parks Canada guidance supersedes the Canada.ca guidance on: Services and information design pattern

Use
  • The symbols are meant to be the main navigation for this page and are your visitor's quick reference to all the activities at the location. We recommend minimizing content that comes before the symbols.
  • Only use Doormats if you have pages that aren't an activity or experience that has an symbol like:
    • Non-symbol activities (ie. Calendar of events)
    • Combined activity pages (ie. Winter activities; Summer activities etc.)
  • Items must be listed in the same order on English and French pages
Maximums
  • Maximum number of doormats on Activities and experiences page: Recommend up to 3 (maximum of 6)
Heading
  • Level: Heading 2
  • Label: Use a heading, when suitable, that describes the distinct content of the doormats. Ex:
    • Trails
    • (Season) activities like: Winter activities
    • Calendar of events
Link text

    Format:

  • Maximum characters:
    • 45 characters (including spaces) for both official languages (optimal)
    • Can extend to 75 characters (including spaces)

    Content:

  • There must not be any duplication between symbols and doormats
    • Ex: A hiking symbol should not also have a hiking doormat that both lead to the same page
Description text

Format:

  • Recommended character limit: 120 characters (including spaces) in both official languages
  • Exception:
    • If description text is longer than 120 characters, this is acceptable as a placeholder for launch. Edit these descriptions to 120 characters as soon as possible.
  • Punctuation:
    • Use oxford commas for lists (in English only)
    • Finish with a period or other punctuation
  • Use action verbs, or list keywords to summarize the information or tasks that can be accomplished on the page

Content:

  • Recommended: review and update seasonally if applicable
  • Descriptive text is standard, with some optional items, and can be adapted to the location as needed
  • Do not separate sentences with line-breaks:

    • Incorrect

    Fees

    Daily visitor fees, camping and program fees, and more.
    Free admission for youth.

    Correct

    Fees

    Daily visitor fees, camping and program fees, and more. Free admission for youth.
